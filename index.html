About Parlekar Police Mitra

In the year 2005 Shri Rajendra Kane transferred to Vile Parle East Police Station as Police Sub-Inspector. Within no time he took a bold initiative to give opportunity to Vile Parle East residents to join hands and help the police force. To his surprise more than 75 residents responded. They all wee from various backgrounds, of course with no criminal record, such as professors, doctors, businessmen, servicemen in private and public sector, hawkers, auto rickshaw drivers, shop owners and nonetheless housewives and college students both boys and girls. This group was then named as "Eagle Brigade".

The major task given to this group was to help police in early morning patrolling to nab chain snatchers who were harassing Parle residents while they go on their morning walks. Within couple of month's time the crime ratio came down and the group members were very happy with the experience of working with policemen. Around 2010, Shri Rajendra Kane was transferred away from the Vile Parle East police station, slowly and sadly all activities carried on by the Eagle Brigade came to a halt.

In the year 2018, Shri Rajendra Kane was transferred to Vile Parle east police station again as a Police Inspector. He once again called the group and told them to start with the same activities. He also suggested that this group should be renamed and hence erstwhile Eagle Brigade started sharing their hands with the Vile Parle East Police as "Parlekar Police Mitra". Parlekar Police Mitra was now involved in various other activities along with early morning patrolling such as;

  1. Nakabandi - Catching two Wheeler riders without a helmet and handing them over to the police force for further suitable action.
  2. Bandobast - Helping police force in Ganpati Visarjan days for controlling traffic and crowd.
  3. Awareness Drive - On 26th January and 15th August rallies were organized by Vile Parle East police station with the help of NCC cadets from Sathe college and Parlekar Police Mitra with holding various types of placards in their hands to make awareness in Parlekar residents regarding safe driving, using seat belts, wearing a helmet, following traffic rules, etc.
  4. Dress Code - All the regular group members were given blue T-shirts and white caps with Parlekar Police Mitra logo and thus given separate identity.
  5. Training - Shri Kane Saheb was not just asking this group to help police force, but he ensured that this group gets trained in every possible manner. He told the group to join one day training of civil defense, fire fighting, and disaster management, which was held in one of the organizations in Vile Parle east.
  6. Be Eyes and Ears of Police - Everybody in the group is told to keep their eyes and ears open while traveling on the road. Any unusual incident must be reported to the police station. Few brave hearts from the group successfully nabbed thieves and law breakers and handed over them to police.
  7. Recognition - Many hard working members received trophies and certificates for their job well done from Vile Parle East Police Station.
